Web 2.0

The transition between the end of Web 1.0 and the beginning of Web 2.0 cannot be exactly determined, since the change was gradual as internet technology became more and more interactive. The term Web 2.0 was first used around the year 2004 to differentiate the current internet technology from the past technology.

Web 2.0 is the beginning of the widespread use and popularity of social media such as Twitter (now X), Facebook, and YouTube. Users can now interact, contribute, and create their own internet space and content, resulting in an explosion of information available to everyone on the internet. The internet became a place where people could communicate, collaborate, and share ideas with others around the world.

Web 2.0 is also known as the "read-write web" because users are no longer limited to reading information. They can also write blogs, upload videos, post pictures, leave comments, react to posts, and participate in online discussions. This level of interaction makes websites more useful and engaging for everyone.

In Web 2.0, web pages are now written using web programming languages such as PHP, Python, Ruby, and Perl. These dynamic programming languages allow websites to respond to users' actions and display updated content instantly. It is now easy to share photos, videos, stories, and opinions through the web.

Popular examples of Web 2.0 websites include Wikipedia, where users can contribute and edit articles; YouTube, where people upload and watch videos; Flickr, where users share photographs; and social networking platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok, where people communicate and share content.

Advantages of Web 2.0

- Encourages communication and collaboration.
- Allows users to create and share content easily.
- Makes information available quickly.
- Supports online learning, business, and entertainment.
- Helps connect people from different parts of the world.

Disadvantages of Web 2.0

- Fake news and misinformation can spread quickly.
- Privacy and security risks.
- Cyberbullying and online harassment.
- Internet addiction and excessive screen time.
